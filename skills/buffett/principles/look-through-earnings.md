> When to read this file: when a company owns meaningful minority stakes in other businesses and reported earnings understate retained investee earnings.

Look-through earnings are a valid Buffett concept, but not a universal company-screening heuristic.

Why it does not belong in the universal ticker pipeline:

- Most operating companies do not have material equity-accounted investees.
- Even when filings mention equity-method investments, they often do not disclose the full set of inputs needed for a reliable calculation.
- The metric is most useful in special situations with explicit ownership percentage, investee earnings, and dividends received.

Practical rule:

- Treat look-through earnings as a situational principle or a caller-supplied calculation.
- Only run it automatically when filings provide all required investee-specific metrics with enough clarity to avoid guesswork.
