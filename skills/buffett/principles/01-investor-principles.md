# Investor Principles

> When to read this file: when deciding how to size positions, when to hold, when to sell, and how to avoid self-inflicted mistakes. These are investor operating principles, not company-screening evaluators.

---

## Focus Investing

Concentrate capital in a small number of businesses you truly understand rather than diversifying for its own sake.

## Patience as Edge

Use time as an advantage. Waiting is often a better decision than forced action.

## Common Behavioral Biases (Psychological Traps in Investing)

Watch for recency bias, impatience, commitment bias, anchoring, and action bias.

## Efficient Market Theory

Treat EMH as a philosophical claim to challenge, not a rule to obey. The point is to stay skeptical of "the market is always right."

## Market Forecasting

Ignore macro forecasts as a decision input. Make decisions from valuation and business quality, not predictions.

## Compounding

Think in decades. Reinvestment and avoiding major mistakes matter more than short bursts of performance.

## Long-term Orientation

Set expectations for multi-year ownership rather than reacting to short-term price movement.

## Independent Thinking

Build a thesis from your own reasoning and evidence, even when it differs from consensus.

## Opportunity Cost Awareness

Every position competes against cash, your hurdle rate, and your next best idea.

## When to Sell (Clear Criteria)

Sell because the thesis broke, valuation became extreme, or a clearly better opportunity exists, not because price moved against you.
